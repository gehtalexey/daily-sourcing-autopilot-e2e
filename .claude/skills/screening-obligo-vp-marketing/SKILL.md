# Position-Specific Screening: obligo-vp-marketing

**Generated from calibration review on 2026-04-12. 10 profiles reviewed with hiring manager.**

## How to Use This File

Read this BEFORE screening any candidate for obligo-vp-marketing. Apply these rules IN ADDITION TO the general screening skill. When these rules conflict with the general screening skill, THESE RULES WIN.

## THE #1 RULE: Full-Scope Marketing Leaders ONLY

**The candidate MUST own the ENTIRE marketing function, not a sub-function.**

**AUTOMATIC REJECT — any title that specifies a marketing sub-function:**
- VP Product Marketing → REJECT
- VP Brand Marketing → REJECT
- VP Performance Marketing → REJECT
- VP Content Marketing → REJECT
- Director of Demand Gen → REJECT (too narrow + too junior)
- Director of Brand → REJECT
- Director of Acquisition Marketing → REJECT
- Senior Director, Performance and Brand Marketing → REJECT
- Head of Growth Marketing → BORDERLINE (depends on scope)

**QUALIFY — titles that signal full marketing ownership:**
- VP Marketing → QUALIFY
- VP, Marketing → QUALIFY
- Head of Marketing → QUALIFY
- VP Marketing / Growth → QUALIFY (growth signal is positive)
- VP Marketing (Growth, GTM) → QUALIFY
- CMO → QUALIFY (if at a startup, not overkill)
- SVP Marketing → QUALIFY

**Why this matters:** The HM wants someone who owns brand + demand gen + product marketing + lifecycle + team. A VP of Brand Marketing at Fiverr is impressive but they only own one piece. Obligo needs the whole-function leader.

## Calibrated Must-Haves (user-validated)

1. **Full-scope marketing ownership** — title must be VP Marketing, Head of Marketing, or CMO (not a sub-function)
2. **10+ years marketing experience** with senior leadership roles
3. **B2B or B2B2C SaaS or fintech background** — at least one relevant company in career
4. **Demand generation track record** — not just brand/comms
5. **NYC-based or willing to relocate** (in-person role)
6. **Tech startup experience** — at least one startup/scale-up in career history

## Calibrated Dealbreakers (user-validated)

- **Sub-function marketing titles** — product marketing, brand marketing, performance marketing, content marketing = automatic reject
- **Single-domain career** — entire career in one non-tech vertical (e.g., 8 years at a healthcare company). Need versatility across industries.
- **Non-tech companies** — government, banks, traditional finance, insurance, CPG, food & beverage, fashion, nonprofit, media companies
- **Unknown tiny companies + freelance/consulting** — VP Marketing at a company nobody has heard of with freelance consulting on the side = not the profile
- **Currently at large enterprise (5000+) with ZERO startup experience** — no startup DNA
- **Sales Operations in title** — muddies the marketing ownership signal

## Search Filter Adjustments

Based on calibration:
1. **Search for "VP Marketing" as exact title** — not just "marketing" in title at VP level (that returns all sub-functions)
2. **Also search for "Head of Marketing", "CMO"** at startup-sized companies
3. **Filter by company headcount 11-1000** — startups and scale-ups only
4. **NYC geo-distance 50 miles** — covers NJ/CT commuters
5. **Exclude candidates from the first search that were sub-function specialists** — the search returned too many Directors of [specific channel]

## Key Takeaway for This Position

**The hiring manager has a very specific profile: full-scope VP Marketing at a tech startup.** Any specialization in the title (brand, product, performance, content) is an automatic reject. The company must be a recognizable tech startup but does NOT need to be fintech — any tech startup with a strong brand works. The biggest mistake is qualifying impressive sub-function leaders (VP Brand at Fiverr, VP Product Marketing at Google) who don't have full marketing ownership experience.
