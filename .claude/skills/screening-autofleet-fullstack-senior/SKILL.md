## Calibrated Must-Haves (user-validated)

1. **5+ years of FULLSTACK experience** -- count years doing fullstack work specifically, NOT total career. If someone was an automation engineer for 4 years then switched to fullstack 1.5 years ago, they do NOT meet the 5+ year requirement.
2. **React + Node.js verifiable in skills or work history** -- must appear in skills list, headline, or experience descriptions. Having them is more important than what the title says.
3. **Israel-based** (hybrid Tel Aviv)
4. **Product company DNA** -- at least one recognized product company in career history
5. **Database experience** (MongoDB, PostgreSQL, MySQL, or similar)

## Calibrated Dealbreakers (user-validated)

- Consulting/outsourcing companies (Develeap, Tikal, Sela, Matrix, Ness, Taldor, Sapiens, Amdocs)
- Banks, government, defense, telcos
- **ALL companies are small/unknown/irrelevant** -- if every company in their history is a tiny unknown shop (e.g., Art3D, ComLogic, Fincom), reject regardless of skills. At least ONE recognized product company is required.
- **Less than 2 years of relevant fullstack experience** -- career switchers (automation, QA, DevOps, data) who recently moved to fullstack with under 2 years are not ready for a senior role
- **Irrelevant education combined with irrelevant companies** -- this combination signals someone outside the Israeli tech ecosystem

## Title Rules -- UPDATED FROM CALIBRATION

**QUALIFY regardless of title if:**
- React AND Node.js (or NestJS/Express) are BOTH in the skills list

- AND 5+ years of relevant engineering experience
- Example: "Senior SE at Palo Alto Networks" with React Hooks + NestJS + MongoDB + PostgreSQL in skills = QUALIFIED (user explicitly approved this pattern)

- Pure frontend title + pure frontend skills + pure frontend companies = reject (e.g., Shani Arnon pattern)

**REJECT if fullstack title BUT experience is too short:**
- "Full Stack Developer" with only 1-2 years of fullstack work (was automation/QA/DevOps before) = reject
- The fullstack title alone is not enough if the relevant experience is thin (e.g., Matan Amitai pattern)
